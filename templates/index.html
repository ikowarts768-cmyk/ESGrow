What is ESGrow

Built for Africa.
Not adapted for it.

Most ESG frameworks are designed for global markets and retrofitted to Africa. ESGrow is different. It was built here, for the companies operating here, using indicators that reflect our realities: land use in the Copperbelt, community impact in the Luapula, governance structures that are uniquely ours.

I first stumbled upon the word ESG in a financial management course. It stood out. I wanted to learn more and build something practical after that. That is how the rationale behind ESGrow came to be. Zambian corporates are increasingly expected to align with global sustainability standards, yet the tools have never been built for this context. ESGrow transforms locally relevant data into strategic intelligence, built for the Zambian corporate environment, not borrowed from somewhere else.

How It Works

From data to score
in three steps.

ESGrow is built on three clear stages from raw company data to ranked, boardroom-ready intelligence.

01
Data Collection

Company ESG data is extracted from published annual reports and structured into {{ indicator_count }} indicators across Environment, Social, and Governance pillars using a standardised scoring template.

{{ total_companies }} Companies Ingested
02
Scoring Engine

A Python-based weighted scoring model processes the data across {{ pillar_count }} pillars, calibrated with sector-specific weights for {{ total_sectors }} Zambian industries, to produce a composite ESGrow score from 0 to 100.

{{ total_sectors }} Sector Weights
03
Live Rankings

Scores are served through a FastAPI backend and rendered as live visual intelligence. Leaderboard rankings, pillar breakdowns and company profiles are all powered by real, verified data.

The Three Pillars

What we
measure.

Each pillar is scored separately then combined into a final weighted ESGrow score, calibrated to the sector each company operates in.

E
Environmental

Climate resilience, land stewardship, water use and emissions. How companies protect the natural resources that sustain African communities from Livingstone to Luapula.

S
Social

People, community investment, labour rights and Ubuntu in practice. How companies treat those who surround and support them, and the broader society they operate within.

G
Governance

Transparency, board accountability, leadership integrity and disclosure quality. The invisible architecture that either builds or destroys long-term trust in any institution.
